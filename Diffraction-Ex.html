$$ I = |U|^2 = \frac{A}{s^2}\cos^2(\delta)\left(\frac{\sin(kul)}{kul}\right)^2$$$$A = s_0^2$$

Where $s_0$ is the distance to the center of the screen from the aperture. $$m = \frac{y}{s'}$$ $$l = \frac{x}{s'}$$ $u$ is the x opening dimensions divided by 2. $$P_0P = \begin{bmatrix} x\\y\\z-r' \end{bmatrix}$$ $$\cos{\delta} = \frac{P_0P}{|P_0P|}\cdot \vec{n}_{screen}$$ "$\delta$ is the angle between the line $P_0P$ and the normal to the screen" [Born and Wolf] $$\vec{n}_{screen} = \begin{bmatrix} 0\\0\\-1 \end{bmatrix}$$ $$\cos{\delta} = \frac{r'-z}{\sqrt{x^2+y^2+(z-r')^2}}$$ $$s' = |P| = \sqrt{x^2+y^2+z^2}$$ $$P = \begin{bmatrix} x\\y\\z \end{bmatrix}$$

$$ I = \frac{s_0^2}{x^2+y^2+z^2}\left(\frac{r'-z}{\sqrt{x^2+y^2+(z-r')^2}}\right)^2\sin\left(\frac{kux}{\sqrt{x^2+y^2+z^2}}\right)\left(\frac{kux}{\sqrt{x^2+y^2+z^2}}\right)^{-1}$$
